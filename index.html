Writing

During my time studying Film at UC Berkeley, I wrote essays exploring the social and personal impact of cinema. My current thesis work at Yonsei focuses on Asian American cinema.

Film Theory & Criticism

Film 170 Wonder in Cinema Into Eternity and The Voyagers — an exploration of the other and inner worlds of humanity, one below ground and one in outerspace. Film 170 Essay #7 The Trader Joe's parking lot as a site of conflict — an observational piece examining competition, space, and human behavior. Film 170 Essay #6 Winged Migration — on the use of medium shots, POV cuts, and suspenseful sound design to evoke empathy across species.

About

I am a graduate film student at Yonsei University in Seoul, South Korea. I recently graduated from the University of California, Berkeley in May 2025 with a Bachelor's Degree in Film and Media.

My enthusiasm revolves around the art of storytelling — particularly expressed through the perspectives of people around the world. My current thesis proposal in graduate school revolves around Asian American cinema.

I have worked across roles including director, cinematographer, editor, and associate producer, spanning documentary, short fiction, and club productions in both the United States and South Korea.
